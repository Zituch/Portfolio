E-Commerce Data Analysis

This project analyzes e-commerce sales data from GameStop, alongside synthetic data created to simulate an online store. The goal is to provide insights into sales trends, customer behavior, and product performance from 2022-2024.

Total Orders: 2000 orders processed

Analysis and Insights

The top-selling product in 2022 was the 'PlayStation Logo T-Shirt,' followed by the 'Pokemon Lugia Plush Game.' The total revenue increased by 20% in 2023, reflecting strong sales growth.

Order status analysis shows that 28.5% of orders were shipped, while 27.5% were still pending. This indicates that there is a need to improve the fulfillment process to reduce pending orders.

Challenges and Learnings

While integrating synthetic data with the GameStop dataset, I encountered challenges in ensuring the consistency and relevance of synthetic data. I had to adjust data formats and verify data integrity to align it with the real data.

One key learning was mastering the use of Power BI for visualizations, particularly in working with different data types and handling complex charting features like stacked bar charts and pie charts.

Conclusion

Through this analysis, we can conclude that certain products, like the PlayStation Logo T-Shirt, are strong performers. Based on customer purchase trends, we could consider focusing marketing efforts on these top-selling products and improving the order fulfillment process for faster deliveries.

Future Improvements

Future improvements include integrating real-time sales data, adding more granular customer demographics, and expanding the analysis to include geographical trends.
